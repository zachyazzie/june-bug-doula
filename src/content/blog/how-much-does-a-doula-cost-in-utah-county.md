Doula pricing varies a lot depending on what's included and how experienced the doula is. Here's a breakdown to help you budget.

## What affects the price

A basic package usually covers a couple of prenatal visits, an on-call window around your due date, birth attendance, and one postpartum check-in. A more comprehensive package adds more prenatal time, an earlier on-call start, extra postpartum support, and sometimes added extras like a calming birth kit or hypno-birth training.

See the current [Basic and Premium birth doula packages](/services/birth-doula/) for exact pricing, or the full [Pricing](/pricing/) page for everything side by side, including the online consultation option if you're not looking for in-person support.

## Can I use my HSA or FSA?

Often, yes - many HSA/FSA plans cover doula support, though it typically requires a Letter of Medical Necessity from your healthcare provider first. It's worth checking with your specific plan administrator before booking, and any doula you hire should be able to give you an itemized receipt for reimbursement. More on this in the [FAQ](/faq/).

## Payment plans

Most doulas, including Junebug, are open to setting up a payment plan rather than requiring the full amount up front - just ask when you reach out.
